About

What I Do

I build systems that listen to machines. Using acoustic emission signals and neural networks, I develop methods to diagnose faults in industrial equipment‚Äîfrom milling machines to pipelines. The goal is simple: catch problems early, prevent failures, save costs.

My approach combines signal processing with modern deep learning architectures. I've worked with CNNs, Transformers, and few-shot learning techniques to handle real-world industrial data where failures are rare but expensive.

Currently funded by the BK21 program at the University of Ulsan, where I'm part of the Industrial AI Lab. Before this, I completed my Bachelor's in Computer Systems Engineering at UET Peshawar.
